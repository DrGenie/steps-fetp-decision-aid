What STEPS does

STEPS helps policy teams explore options for scaling Field Epidemiology Training Programs in India. It links three inputs:

  • Preferences from a discrete choice experiment with stakeholders.
  • Costing templates from WHO, NIE and NCDC for Frontline, Intermediate and Advanced programmes.
  • Epidemiological multipliers that translate training scale into indicative public health benefits.

Configure a programme, view endorsement, costs and benefits, compare scenarios and export briefings.

How to use the tool

  1. Open Configuration and set programme design, cost, cohort size and number of cohorts.
  2. Choose the preference model: average mixed logit or supportive latent class group.
  3. Click Apply configuration to update all results and View results summary for a modal snapshot.
  4. Save scenarios in Saved scenarios and export to Excel or a policy brief PDF.
  5. Use Advanced & methods to adjust multipliers for workshops. All calculations remain in INR.

STEPS is responsive and can be used in workshops with central and state partners.

Attributes and levels in the FETP preference study

The study asked stakeholders to choose between alternative FETP programme designs and an opt out option. STEPS keeps configurations inside the experimental range so results remain consistent with the models.

Program design attributes

Attribute Levels used in the experiment Explanation
Program tier Frontline (3 months); Intermediate (12 months); Advanced (24 months) Indicates whether the programme is Frontline, Intermediate or Advanced. Duration reflects typical training and field work length.
Career incentive Government and partner certificate; University qualification; Government career pathway Shows the main career benefit for completing the programme. Incentives affect attractiveness to trainees and organisations.
Mentorship intensity Low (≥5 fellows per mentor); Medium (3–4 per mentor); High (≤2 per mentor) Captures hands on mentoring support. Higher intensity is more attractive and typically more costly.
Delivery mode Blended; Fully in person; Fully online Describes how teaching is delivered. Blended combines classroom, online and field components.
Expected response time Within 30 days; Within 15 days; Within 7 days Summarises how quickly graduates help detect and respond to outbreaks or public health events.
Cost per trainee per month 75,000; 100,000; 250,000; 350,000; 400,000 INR Total programme cost per trainee per month. The tool keeps the slider in this range.

Opt out and cost templates

Element Definition How STEPS uses it
Opt out alternative Status quo where no new FETP training is funded in the scenario. Used as the benchmark. Endorsement is the predicted share choosing the programme rather than opting out.
Cost templates Budget templates from WHO, NIE and NCDC for each tier. Templates are alternatives. Switching template updates structure and totals. Opportunity cost flows into economic cost when toggled on.
Opportunity cost of trainee time Value of trainee time during training. When toggled on, added to programme cost to show total economic cost.
Preference models Average mixed logit and a supportive latent class group. Switching model updates endorsement and willingness to pay values for sensitivity analysis.

About STEPS

The University of Newcastle is developing a business case for strengthening and scaling FETP in India. STEPS supports structured exploration of scale up options and clear communication of cost and benefit implications.

  • How do costs and benefits change when moving from Frontline to Intermediate or Advanced tiers.
  • What happens to endorsement when mentorship intensity is increased.
  • How many cohorts and trainees are needed to reach target scale.

Prepared by Mesfin Genie, PhD, Newcastle Business School, The University of Newcastle, Australia. Contact mesfin.genie@newcastle.edu.au.
